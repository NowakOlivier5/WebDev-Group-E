Animal Marine Life

UN Sustainable Development Goal 14, "Life Below Water," is all about taking care of our oceans and marine life. It focuses on big threats like overfishing, plastic pollution, harmful chemicals, and habitat loss from coastal building.

Make a difference today

Everyday people can make a real difference for marine wildlife and help out with UN Goal 14 by making a few easy changes. Cut down on plastic by using reusable bags, bottles, and straws to keep oceans cleaner. Always throw away trash the right way and recycle to stop waste from ending up in the water. Choose sustainable seafood to avoid overfishing. Get involved in beach cleanups or volunteer for conservation groups. Share what you know about marine issues and support ocean-friendly policies. Small steps, done regularly, can add up to big results for marine life.



Consequences

Oceans, seas and marine resources are increasingly threatened, degraded or destroyed by human activities, reducing their ability to provide crucial ecosystem services. Important classes of threats are, among others, climate change, marine pollution, unsustainable extraction of marine resources and physical alterations and destruction of marine and coastal habitats and landscapes. The deterioration of coastal and marine ecosystems and habitats is negatively affecting human well-being worldwide.
